# tsusiat

> Tsusiat Falls, a prominent feature on the southwestern coast of Vancouver
Island, is traditionally known to the Huu-ay-aht First Nations as Tsosayii-aht,
meaning "waterfall split in two by a rock".

## FAQ

1. Where did the name tsusiat come from?

   It's a [beautiful][tsusiat-falls] waterfall on British Columbia's [Pacific
   Rim National Park][pacific-rim] that also hosts a campground part of the
   [West Coast Trail][west-coast-trail].
